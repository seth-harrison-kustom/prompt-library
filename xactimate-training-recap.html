Concepts Worth Keeping

the through-lines of the whole series
01 The price list is a language
“If it’s not a component in the line item, it’s not in the line item.” Read the components before you argue the price.
02 The three pricing levers
Direct yield · supporting events · labor trade code. Change any one and the price moves — this is how estimators reason about any line item.
03 Sketch drives everything
Accurate variables (WC, W, F, PC) feed dozens of line items automatically. Sketch first, scope second.
04 Margin ≠ markup
10 & 10 O&P is a markup on cost, not a margin on revenue. It nets 16.667% — not 20%.
05 ACV vs RCV
RCV is full replacement at today’s prices; ACV is RCV minus depreciation. Most estimates are built to RCV.
06 Documentation is the game
Documentation is what gets us paid; communication is what gets customers in the door. Write it so the adjuster doesn’t have to think.
07 Know the program rules first
Pull the current CE SOP guideline before estimating, not after. Every program has items it requires — and items it won’t pay.
08 Right report, right audience
Reports are golf clubs — pick the one that fits the shot. Carrier, field, and homeowner each see a different version.
