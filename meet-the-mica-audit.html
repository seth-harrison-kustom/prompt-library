Meet the MICA Compliance Audit

When a mitigation estimate request comes in, an automated review reads the MICA file, checks it against our drying & documentation standards, and drops a plain-language punch list right onto the DASH job — before the estimate is written.

Runs on every mit estimate request Read-only — nothing is changed in MICA Lands as a DASH note, tagged to you

The idea in one line

A system that has your back — so great work doesn't get slowed down.

Mitigation files miss things — a day of readings, a stop time, an unbuilt sketch. Those gaps tend to surface late, bounce the estimate back, and cost everyone days. This audit reads the file the moment the request comes in and surfaces anything worth a second look, in time to square it away before the estimate is written. It's built to give the team a head start, not grade anyone — a reviewer that reads the file first so nothing gets stuck downstream.

How to read it

Every item is one of three things.

● FIX

Fix before the estimate

Something's missing or off that will block or distort the estimate. Handle it first.

● VERIFY

Confirm or explain

Looks off, but there may be a good reason. Confirm it's right — or note why.

● READY

Good to pull from

What already checks out, laid out so the estimator can lift it straight into the estimate.

The #1 mismatch — and its fix

Audit flagging something you know you did? Re-export the MICA file.

If the results don't match what you know you documented in MICA — the readings, the sketch, the stop times are there on your tablet — the cause is almost always the same: the export never fully landed. It can look done on the tablet but never make it into the MICA website; the export hiccuped, failed, or didn't run. The audit reads the website, so it sees the older state.

When that's the case: re-export from MICA, confirm it goes through, and those "missing" items resolve — they were done, they just hadn't synced. Then use MICA FIXED: (below) to have the audit re-check the file and confirm it's clean.

What it actually checks — and why

Four areas, all read straight from the MICA file.

Loss & linkage

Is it the right file, and is it connected?

  • The MICA loss exists and isn't a duplicate or an unmerged split.
  • The DASH↔Mitigate link is in place so the loss is reachable from the job.
  • The file was exported to DASH — the work actually left MICA (and when, and by whom).

Did it dry — and can it bill?

The drying story has to hold up.

  • Readings every day of the equipment window, weekends included — a day with no readings can't bill.
  • Grain depression above 5 mid-job — proof the chamber is actually pulling moisture.
  • Materials hit their dry goal on the moisture map.
  • Dehu & air movers sized to MICA's numbers (oversized? just document why).

Is it documented?

The estimate is only as good as the file.

  • Source & Type of Loss set; Category / Class of water on every room.
  • Sketch built and equipment placed — MICA figures every min/max and dehu size from the sketch, so an empty one throws off the whole scope.
  • Equipment stopped (no stop time = no billable runtime) and control dates filled.
  • Photos & notes where they belong.

Context & edge cases

It knows when the normal rules don't apply.

  • Passes through MICA's own Process Audit flags, de-duplicated against ours.
  • Preliminary requests get a "file expected incomplete" banner — not a defect list.
  • T&M jobs are skipped — those belong on the T&M form, not here.
The most important part

Talk back to it

You steer it — with a DASH note that tags CE.

The audit gets smarter, re-checks itself, and runs on demand because you tell it to — no ticket, no waiting. You do it the way you already work: a note on the DASH job. There are exactly three headers, and they all work the same simple way:

The whole rule → Put a DASH note on the job, tag Centralized Estimating, and start it with one of these three headers. The CE tag is what routes it in — no CE tag, nothing happens.
MICA FEEDBACK:

A flag was wrong or confusing — tell us what's actually true.

MICA FIXED:

You re-exported / fixed the file — re-check it and post fresh results.

MICA RUN:

Run the audit any time — even a job that never had a request form.

MICA FEEDBACK: — it got something wrong

It's a v1 rule set; it will occasionally miss the mark. When it does, correct it directly and it gets logged against the job and reviewed fast — real corrections have gone from feedback to fixed the same day.

MICA FEEDBACK: the grain depression flag was wrong — GD was above 5 on the 17th and 18th. Everything else looked right.

Wrong flag?

Tell us what was actually true. We tighten the rule.

Confusing wording?

Say so. We rewrite it for the field.

Missing something?

Tell us what a good reviewer would've caught. We add it.

Fixed it? Re-run it — MICA FIXED:

Re-exported and squared the file away? You won't have to wait for the next request or chase anyone down. Start a note with MICA FIXED: (tagging Centralized Estimating, same as feedback) and the audit re-reads the file and posts fresh results — so you can confirm it's actually clean now.

MICA FIXED: re-exported — readings and sketch are in now, please re-check.

Want it run any time — MICA RUN:

Need an audit on a job that never went through a request form, or just want a fresh check on demand? Start a note with MICA RUN: (tagging Centralized Estimating) and the audit runs and posts results — no need to have "fixed" anything.

MICA RUN: please run the MICA audit on this job.

Three headers, one rule: MICA FEEDBACK: (a flag was wrong), MICA FIXED: (fixed it, re-check), MICA RUN: (run it now) — each just needs the header and a Centralized Estimating tag on the note.

Set expectations straight

What this is — and isn't.

✓ A support tool

It's here to help the team turn out complete files fast — a head start on the review, not extra homework.

✓ Read-only

It never changes anything in MICA. It reads, reports, and tags — that's it.

✕ Not a billing review

It's not second-guessing dollars. It's making sure the file is right and complete.

✕ Not a gotcha

No scores, no shaming on any single job. Over time we may share trends with GMs — but only to help a team that's consistently getting stuck, never to call out a person.
