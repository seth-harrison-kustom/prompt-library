Value

ACV vs. RCV & Depreciation

Two ways to state what a damaged item is worth. Carriers and adjusters switch between them constantly — you need to follow which one is being discussed.

RCV
Replacement Cost Value — what it costs to replace the damaged item with new, at today's prices.
ACV
Actual Cash Value — RCV minus depreciation.
Depreciation
The reduction in value from age, wear, and condition of the damaged item.
Depreciation can be recoverable — held back, then paid out after repairs are complete and invoiced — or non-recoverable, which is never paid.

Most Xactimate estimates are built to RCV — the full replacement cost. You'll see depreciation fields on estimate reports; you don't need to set or manage them for this training — just know what they represent and which number the adjuster is talking about.
The GC coordination fee

O&P — Overhead & Profit

The general contractor's coordination fee, added on top of the direct costs in the estimate.

  • Set in the Parameters tab — typically 10% overhead / 10% profit ("10 and 10").
  • O&P applies on top of the direct costs in the estimate — it is not baked into the unit prices.
  • When it's appropriate: when a general contractor is coordinating two or more subcontracted trades.
  • Some program guidelines restrict or limit O&P — always check before including it.

What "overhead" actually covers (per Verisk)

Verisk's price list is built without general O&P, job-related O&P, or sales tax in the unit prices — those get added after the line items. Verisk recognizes three overhead categories:

General
Business-operation costs not tied to any one job — office rent, utilities, admin salaries, licenses, advertising. This is the "O" in the O&P you add in Parameters.
Job-related
Tied to a project but not a task — project managers, portable offices/restrooms, temp power & fencing, security. Added as separate line items, not the O&P %.
Job-personnel
The cost of the people doing the work (vehicles, phones, tool depreciation) — already inside the labor portion of each unit price. When work is subcontracted this is "subcontractor O&P," and it's baked into labor, not the GC O&P line.
Takeaway: the "O&P" you toggle in Parameters is the general contractor's overhead + profit for coordinating the job — a markup on the whole estimate. Subcontractor O&P is a different thing already living in the labor rates.
The number everyone gets wrong

The Margin vs. Markup Problem

10% overhead + 10% profit sounds like 20%. It is not a 20% margin. These are markups on cost, not a margin on revenue.

markup = profit ÷ cost   |   margin = profit ÷ revenue   |   margin = markup ÷ (1 + markup)

Why it matters: "10 and 10" O&P is a 20% markup on cost, which is only a 16.67% margin on revenue. Contractors building a business model around insurance work have to price against the margin, not the markup — the gap is real money.
Off-price-list line items

Bid Items

Line items entered at a fixed, contractor-determined price rather than the Xactimate price list.

  • Used when the price list doesn't carry the item, or a real subcontractor/vendor bid sets the price.
  • Each bid item has a per-line O&P setting. When the estimate carries O&P, bid items typically pick it up like any other line — and if one isn't, you just flip the O&P toggle on for that line.
  • Knowing that toggle exists helps you sanity-check that a bid item is being treated the way you intend.
Bottom line: O&P on a bid item is a per-line setting you control — not an automatic exclusion. If a bid item should carry O&P and isn't, turn it on.
Also on the Parameters tab

Labor Efficiencies

Labor is the largest variable in construction. Xactimate adjusts labor productivity by job size and conditions through a labor-efficiency model — chosen on the Parameters tab.

Restoration / Remodel / Service
Default. Finished structures, often occupied, coordinated schedules. Prices include drive time, mobilization, material delivery, and the productivity hit of matching existing work. Fits the vast majority of estimates.
Large Restoration / Remodel
New option (Jan 2026 price lists). Larger jobs that are easily accessible and separated from finished areas, usually unoccupied. Sits between the default and Total Rebuild.
Total Rebuild or Similar
Replaces "New Construction." Very large, freely accessible, unoccupied — ground-up rebuilds. Most efficient labor productivity.

Labor minimums

Peripheral time (drive, pick up materials, set up/tear down) is averaged into normal unit prices — but on a tiny task it dominates. A 1-sq-ft drywall patch might be 45 minutes of work but 2–3 hours all-in. So trades carry a minimum labor charge; each efficiency setting can auto-apply it, and Xactimate tops up the labor on the task until the minimum is met.

Pick the efficiency that matches reality: even a large job with scaffolding, hard access, and complex scheduling may belong in the default model, not a higher-efficiency one. The wrong model quietly moves the labor number.
