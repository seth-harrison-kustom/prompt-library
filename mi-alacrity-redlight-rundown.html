Why "Contractor Estimating" ≠ "the estimator is the holdup"

"Contractor Estimating" is a phase, not a diagnosis. A job lands in it the moment it's assigned — before anyone has been onsite — and the 7/20–21 "deadline" is an auto-set program clock that fires whether or not the job has been inspected. So what's actually driving the "red" and the Alacrity shut-off threat?

Not estimating throughput. It's two things, neither of which is "our estimators are behind":

  • Program SLA timers running on jobs we can't inspect. Homeowner-unresponsive, tree-not-removed, and mitigation-still-running jobs sit accruing overdue time. Every estimate-stage claim is flagged in AlacNet as "kustom_revision" with an expired timer (8–71 days) — that's the exact metric Alacrity uses to threaten shut-off.
  • Supplement / revision cycles. After a program rejection, the clock is 24 hours to resubmit. Supplements (Vega, Wisner, McMahon) and EO revisions (Trail) generate rejection/resubmit churn — and CE is turning them (Vega + Trail both cleared 7/23).

Vega-Narvaez shows an AlacNet "Non-Submittal — Overdue" flag on 7/19: the revised supplement wasn't resubmitted within the program's 24-hour window. That window ran while the team was waiting on the contractor's flooring bid. Worth a quick confirm with Caldwell/Graham/Villarreal on whether that was purely the bid wait or a handoff we can tighten — it's the only spot on all 11 that touches an internal timing miss.

Terminology — so we're all reading the board the same way
Ball → CE (Centralized Estimating)
Rejection / Revision-Requested — the program bounces a submitted estimate back to CE to review & revise. The estimate exists; this is a fix, not "no estimate yet." 24h to resubmit.
Ball → MI local office
Withdrawal — the Michigan team pulls off the job (customer canceled, duplicate/superseded assignment, out of scope). A local decision, not an estimating action.
Program SLA clock
Non-Submittal / Overdue — the program's timer fired because a file wasn't submitted in the required window. Often driven by waiting on an upstream input (a bid, a scan), not an idle estimator.
Ball → MI production / field
"Need More Info" (in estimating) — the estimator is blocked waiting on the field team for the bid, Matterport, photos, or a scope decision. "In estimating" but not on the estimator.
